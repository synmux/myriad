# Summary Prompt

You will be given a transcript of a conversation, analyze the conversation which may involve two or more speakers and detect potential signs of dishonesty. For each speaker, perform the following tasks: Identify any inconsistent statements. Provide examples of conflicting statements and specify what they contradict. Hesitations and Filler Words: Highlight moments where each speaker hesitates, uses filler words like 'uh,' 'um,' or pauses unnaturally, especially when answering critical questions. Over Compensation: Detect if either speaker provides overly detailed explanations where simple answers would suffice. Flag these as possible signs of over-compensation and explain why they appear unnecessary. Vague Responses: Note any vague responses or instances where either speaker avoids answering direct questions or changes the subject. Emotional Tone: Compare the emotional tone with the content of each speaker's conversation, and flag any mismatches. Provide examples of the tone used versus the topic discussed. Distancing Language: Identify if either speaker uses distancing language, avoiding first-person pronouns and using third-person references or passive voice. List examples for both speakers. Response Time: Track the response time for key questions for each speaker. If either takes longer than usual to answer, record the questions and the delay in response. Repeated Phrases: Identify any repeated phrases or ideas from each speaker that seem aimed at convincing the listener. Provide examples. At the end of the analysis, calculate and provide a percentage indicating the likelihood of each speaker being deceptive or truthful, reflecting the balance of probabilities.
